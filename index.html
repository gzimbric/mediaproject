Deforestation

Deforestation is the permanent destruction of forests in order to make
the land available for other uses. Each year about 18 million acres of forest
are lost, roughly the size of the country Panama.

  • Tell Me More

To date, about half of the world's tropical
forests have been cleared
It's only getting worse.

In the last century alone, Indonesia has lost 15.79 million hectares of forest land
due to common needs like paper, furniture, and even homes. Forests are also typically cleared to make
more room for urbanization of large ciies in desperate need of expansion
Not to mention that most trees are cut illegally for fuel and energy.

Trees aren't the only thing effected by deforestation.

Deforestation effects many things other than just trees. One of the biggest effects of deforestation is driving and destroying habitats of many different species. Deforestation can also effect the temperature in forests as without trees animals and plants loose the essential shade they need. Another effect is global warming, as fewer forests means increased greenhouse gases in our atmosphere.

How you can help

Recycle

From the store to home, always look for ways to include recycling. For example, look for recycled products when shopping. These products usually don't derive from things like deforestation.

Go Paperless

Using as little paper as possible helps stop the need to cut down more trees. In today's world, we have sufficient technology to go all electronic- ranging from things like bills to reports.

Reforestation

If there is ever the need to cut down a tree, plant a new one somewhere else where it can grow to be an adult. If we were all to replant one tree for every one we cut down then deforestion wouldn't be a problem.

Effected by deforestation

Central America

Today, only about 2/5ths of Central America's forests remain. Unfortunately, a major cause of deforestation there is for landing strips and roads for drug trafficking.

Madagascar

Madagascar's expanding population had left the country with a small supply of fresh water, food, and sanitation in most areas. Deforestation has led to loosing over 80% of Madagascar's rainforests, also leading to desertification and water degradation.

Brazil

Most of the deforestation in Brazil is taking place in the Amazon. This is mostly due to rise in development in agriculture as more and more room is needed for fields.

Honduras

At one point Honduras was virtually covered with trees, however today only about half of that remains thanks to deforestation. In 1990-2005 alone Honduras saw a 37% decline in it's forest cover.

Ready to do something?

Deforestation is a serious issue and won't get any better by ignoring it.
